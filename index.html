About

Biography


Francisco Cornellana is a travel and wildlife photographer based in the UK, with over a decade of work spanning six continents and some of the world's most extraordinary landscapes.

His images have taken him from the glaciated fjords of Alaska and the volcanic shores of Hawaii to the Arctic archipelago of Lofoten, the savannahs of Kenya and the granite towers of Patagonia. Whether tracking predators on the Masai Mara, shooting long exposures on a Norwegian coastline at 2 a.m., or documenting the culture and architecture of Morocco's ancient medinas, Francisco brings the same methodical eye and technical precision to every subject.

His specialty disciplines — aerial photography with DJI systems, macro photography, and long-exposure slow-motion work — complement a broader body of travel and landscape work that spans every major photographic environment: desert, arctic, jungle, ocean, mountain and urban.

Francisco works with the Sony Alpha system, including the 61-megapixel Sony A7R IV and the Sony A1, paired with a full suite of Sony G Master lenses. His work has been exhibited privately and is available for editorial, commercial and personal licensing.

Tools

My Gear

Gear matters, though it is not everything. The right tool removes friction between the vision and the image — it does not create the vision. After years of testing systems across demanding conditions, I have settled on a kit that handles everything from telephoto wildlife bursts at 30 frames per second to low-light long exposures on a tripod at minus twenty degrees.

Camera bodies: Sony Alpha A1 — 50 MP, 30fps, world-class autofocus for birds and fast action. Sony Alpha A7R IV — 61 MP for landscape and architecture work where resolution is paramount.

Lenses: Sony 100–400mm f/4.5–5.6 GM (wildlife and airshows). Sony 16–35mm f/2.8 GM (landscape and architecture). Sony 24–105mm f/4 G (travel and reportage). Sony 90mm f/2.8 Macro G (macro work). Sony 200–600mm f/5.6–6.3 G (long-distance wildlife).

Aerial: DJI Mavic 3 Pro — used for the drone gallery and aerial work across multiple locations.

Support: Gitzo carbon fibre tripod with Arca-Swiss ballhead for long exposures. Peak Design travel system for field work.
